'''Amos Le Blanc''' is a Canadian film director and [[cinematographer]] based in [[Los Angeles]] and [[Toronto]]. He is known primarily for music video direction and commercial production. He has twice won the Young Director Award at the [[Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity]], winning in 2015 for Thugli's "Run This" and again the following year for Young Empires' "The Gates". At the 2014 MuchMusic Video Awards, Le Blanc won Director of the Year in his first year of eligibility, a result that drew attention partly because he was also nominated against himself in other categories with different videos from the same year. In 2019 he won in the Film category at [[One Club for Creativity|The One Club for Creativity's]] Young Guns 17.

==Early life and education==
Le Blanc was born in 1990 in [[Kitchener, Ontario]].{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}} He has described first picking up a camera at age eleven, shooting and editing skateboard and snowboard videos and filming live concerts for touring musicians.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} By the age of eighteen he was working professionally as a cinematographer on music videos for Canadian artists.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}

He subsequently enrolled at [[Sheridan College]] in [[Oakville, Ontario]], studying Media Arts, where he transitioned from cinematography to directing.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}}

==Career==

===Early work===
Le Blanc's first documented credit as a director of photography on a widely released music video was for the Islands track "This Is Not A Song", premiered by [[Stereogum]] in December 2011.{{sfn|Stereogum|2011}} The video was directed by Johnny Nghiem; Le Blanc handled cinematography.

In 2013 he directed several music videos within the Toronto electronic music scene, including Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation". He also served as producer on Keys N Krates' "Treat Me Right", directed by Ohji Inoue, released on Dim Mak Records in March 2013 and premiered by Annie Mac on BBC Radio 1 on February 8, 2013.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} The single reached number one on the Hype Machine and number one on Beatport's Electronica Top 100 chart upon release.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} In June 2013 the video was included in the 23rd Saatchi and Saatchi New Directors' Showcase at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity; ''Ad Age'' covered the event and named the directors directly.{{sfn|Ad Age|2013}}

He co-directed "Dum Dee Dum" for Keys N Krates with collaborator slavelabour.co.{{sfn|VideoStatic|2013}} He also co-directed Thugli's "Run This" with Ohji Inoue through Vision Film Company.

===2014 MuchMusic Video Awards===

Le Blanc's work from 2013 produced an unusual result at the 2014 MuchMusic Video Awards. He won Director of the Year for Thugli's "Run This" (co-directed with Inoue), and Dance Video of the Year for Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation".{{sfn|EDM Canada|2014}}{{sfn|Newswire.ca|2014}} "Run This" was also nominated in additional categories, meaning Le Blanc effectively competed against himself on the ballot. Both videos had been produced in cooperation with MuchFACT.

Keys N Krates' "Dum Dee Dum" received a Grand Prize nomination at the Prism Prize and an Official Selection at the Berlin Music Video Awards in the same period.

===Young Director Award===

In 2015, Le Blanc and Inoue won First Prize in the Music Video North America category at the Young Director Award, presented annually at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity.{{sfn|Young Director Award|2015}} The award was for Thugli's "Run This". The video was also shortlisted that year for the 1.4 Brilliant Filmmaking Award, which received over 500 entries.{{sfn|OnepointFour|2015}}

Le Blanc won the Young Director Award a second time for Young Empires' "The Gates" (2015). This second win was referenced in press coverage around August 2016, when he signed with The Sweet Shop for international commercial representation.{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

===The Sweet Shop and commercial work===

In August 2016, production company The Sweet Shop announced Le Blanc's signing for international commercial work. The announcement was covered by LBBOnline, shots magazine, and adobo Magazine, among others.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} Managing Director Laura Thoel of The Sweet Shop Los Angeles was quoted in shots describing his work as "innovative, clever, beautiful and raw."{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

His commercial clients have included Mercedes-Benz, Tesla, Disney, American Express, Budweiser, Air Canada, Beats by Dre, Canon, McDonald's, and Sportchek.

===Young Guns===

In November 2019 Le Blanc won in the Film category at the One Club for Creativity's Young Guns 17 awards, held at Sony Hall in New York.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}} The program selected 28 winners from 18 countries across more than 45 countries of submission, judged by a panel of 61 industry figures.{{sfn|SHOOTonline|2019}} Le Blanc was affiliated with Dream Inc., the Toronto-based creative company he co-founded.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}} LBBOnline published a dedicated article on the win.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2019}}

===Fridays for Future===

In September 2021, Le Blanc directed "The Denial", a climate-denial PSA for [[Fridays for Future]] U.S., released through the agency Fred and Farid Los Angeles for the Global Climate Strike. The spot was covered by shots, LBBOnline, Campaign US, and adobo Magazine.{{sfn|shots|2021}}{{sfn|LBBOnline|2021b}}{{sfn|Campaign US|2021}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2021}} In March 2022, he directed a follow-up campaign for Fridays for Future U.S., "We Don't Care", released to announce the March 25 Global Climate Strike.{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2022}}

===Music performance===

Le Blanc was a member of the Kitchener, Ontario punk rock four-piece Mockingbird Wish Me Luck, which signed to [[Dine Alone Records|Dine Alone Music]] in 2010.{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}} The label re-released the band's debut EP ''Goodbye Debris'' digitally in 2010 through Dine Alone Music Inc. Le Blanc contributed as writer, producer, guitarist, and vocalist.

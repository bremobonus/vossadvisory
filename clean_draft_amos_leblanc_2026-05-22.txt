'''Amos Le Blanc''' is a Canadian film director and cinematographer based in [[Los Angeles]] and [[Toronto]]. He is known primarily for music video direction and commercial production, and has twice won the Young Director Award at [[Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity|Cannes Lions]]—in 2015 for Thugli's "Run This" and again the following year for Young Empires' "The Gates". At the 2014 MuchMusic Video Awards, Le Blanc won Director of the Year in his first year of eligibility, a result that drew attention partly because he was also nominated against himself in other categories with different videos from the same year.

==Early life and education==
Le Blanc was born in 1990 in [[Kitchener, Ontario]].{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}} He has described first picking up a camera at age eleven, shooting and editing skateboard and snowboard videos and filming live concerts for touring musicians.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} By the age of eighteen he was working professionally as a cinematographer on music videos for Canadian artists.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}

He subsequently enrolled at [[Sheridan College]] in [[Oakville, Ontario]], studying Media Arts, where he transitioned from cinematography to directing.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}}

==Career==

===Early work===
Le Blanc's first documented credit as a director of photography on a widely released music video was for the Islands track "This Is Not A Song", premiered by [[Stereogum]] in December 2011.{{sfn|Stereogum|2011}} The video was directed by Johnny Nghiem; Le Blanc handled cinematography.

In 2013 he directed several music videos in quick succession, largely within the Toronto electronic music scene. Among them were Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation", which went viral in part for its dodgeball-themed choreography. He also served as producer on Keys N Krates' "Treat Me Right", directed by Ohji Inoue, released on Dim Mak Records in March 2013 and premiered by Annie Mac on BBC Radio 1 on February 8, 2013.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} The single reached number one on Hype Machine and number one on Beatport's Electronica Top 100 chart upon release.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} In June 2013 the video was included in the 23rd Saatchi & Saatchi New Directors' Showcase at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, one of nineteen directors selected globally; Ad Age covered the event and named Inoue and the video directly.{{sfn|Ad Age|2013}}{{sfn|Saatchi & Saatchi|2013}} He co-directed "Dum Dee Dum" for Keys N Krates,{{sfn|VideoStatic|2013}} and "Run This" for Thugli with co-director Ohji Inoue through Vision Film Company.

===2014 MuchMusic Video Awards===

Le Blanc's work from 2013 produced an unusual MMVA result the following year. He won Director of the Year for Thugli's "Run This" (co-directed with Inoue), and Dance Video of the Year for Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation".{{sfn|EDM Canada|2014}}{{sfn|Newswire.ca|2014}} "Run This" was also nominated in additional categories, meaning Le Blanc effectively competed against himself on the ballot. Both videos had been produced in cooperation with MuchFACT.

Keys N Krates' "Dum Dee Dum" received a Grand Prize nomination at the Prism Prize and an Official Selection at the Berlin Music Video Awards in the same period.

===Young Director Award===

In 2015, Le Blanc and Inoue won First Prize in the Music Video North America category at the Young Director Award, presented annually at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity.{{sfn|Young Director Award|2015}} The award was for Thugli's "Run This", produced by Vision Film. The video was also shortlisted that year for the 1.4 Brilliant Filmmaking Award, which received over 500 entries.{{sfn|OnepointFour|2015}}

Le Blanc won the Young Director Award a second time for Young Empires' "The Gates" (2015). The second win was referenced in press coverage around August 2016, when he signed with The Sweet Shop for international commercial representation.{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

===The Sweet Shop and commercial work===

In August 2016, production company The Sweet Shop announced Le Blanc's signing for international commercial work. The announcement was covered by LBBOnline, shots magazine, and adobo Magazine, among others.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} Managing Director Laura Thoel of The Sweet Shop Los Angeles was quoted in shots describing his work as "innovative, clever, beautiful and raw."{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

His commercial clients have included Mercedes-Benz, Tesla, Disney, American Express, Budweiser, Air Canada, Beats by Dre, Canon, McDonald's, and Sportchek.

In 2017 he directed the music video for Rudimental featuring James Arthur, "[[Sun Comes Up]]", filmed in South Africa. The video accumulated over 34.6 million views on YouTube.

===Young Guns===

After what he later described as a period of roughly three years living and working in Southeast Asia, Le Blanc returned to North American work and in November 2019 won in the Film category at the One Club for Creativity's Young Guns 17 awards, held at Sony Hall in New York.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}} The program selected 28 winners from 18 countries, judged by a panel of 61 industry figures.{{sfn|SHOOTonline|2019}} Le Blanc was affiliated with Dream Inc., the Toronto-based creative company he co-founded.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}}

LBBOnline published a dedicated article on the win.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2019}}

===Director aliases and production company===
Beyond credits under his given name, Le Blanc has worked under two recurring billing forms. The directorial collective '''slavelabour.co''' (used by Le Blanc with rotating collaborators) holds the directing credit on the Keys N Krates video for "Dum Dee Dum" (2013){{sfn|VideoStatic|2013}} and the Thugli video for "Sic Em" (2016). His production company '''dream inc.''' (also branded online as thedream.io) holds production credits on projects including Thugli's "Run This" (2013) and his commercial work with Fridays for Future.

===Music performance and songwriting===
Le Blanc was a member of the Kitchener, Ontario punk rock four-piece '''Mockingbird Wish Me Luck''', which signed to [[Dine Alone Records|Dine Alone Music]] in 2010.{{sfn|Dine Alone Records|nd}}{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}} The label re-released the band's debut EP ''Goodbye Debris'' digitally in 2010 through Dine Alone Music Inc. (distributed by The Orchard).{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}} Le Blanc contributed as writer, producer, guitarist, and vocalist. A follow-up 7" single ''Branches'' (catalogue DAV026, limited to 500 black vinyl) was released on Dine Alone Records on December 14, 2010.{{sfn|Dine Alone Store|2010}}

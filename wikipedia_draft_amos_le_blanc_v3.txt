'''Amos Le Blanc''' is a Canadian film director and cinematographer based in [[Los Angeles]] and [[Toronto]]. He is known primarily for music video direction and commercial production, and has twice won the Young Director Award at [[Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity|Cannes Lions]]—in 2015 for Thugli's "Run This" and again the following year for Young Empires' "The Gates". At the 2014 MuchMusic Video Awards, Le Blanc won Director of the Year in his first year of eligibility, a result that drew attention partly because he was also nominated against himself in other categories with different videos from the same year.

==Early life and education==
Le Blanc was born in 1990 in [[Kitchener, Ontario]].{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}} He has described first picking up a camera at age eleven, shooting and editing skateboard and snowboard videos and filming live concerts for touring musicians.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} By the age of eighteen he was working professionally as a cinematographer on music videos for Canadian artists.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}

He subsequently enrolled at [[Sheridan College]] in [[Oakville, Ontario]], studying Media Arts, where he transitioned from cinematography to directing.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}}

==Career==

===Early work===
Le Blanc's first documented credit as a director of photography on a widely released music video was for the Islands track "This Is Not A Song", premiered by [[Stereogum]] in December 2011.{{sfn|Stereogum|2011}} The video was directed by Johnny Nghiem; Le Blanc handled cinematography.


In 2013 he directed five music videos in quick succession, largely within the Toronto electronic music scene. Among them were Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation", which went viral in part for its dodgeball-themed choreography. He also served as producer on Keys N Krates' "Treat Me Right", directed by Ohji Inoue, released on Dim Mak Records in March 2013 and premiered by Annie Mac on BBC Radio 1 on February 8, 2013.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} The single reached number one on Hype Machine and number one on Beatport's Electronica Top 100 chart upon release.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} The video was selected as a Vimeo Staff Pick, appeared in Vimeo's Top 10 Videos of 2013,{{sfn|Vimeo|2013}} and was selected by NOWNESS. In June 2013 the video was included in the 23rd Saatchi & Saatchi New Directors' Showcase at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, one of nineteen directors selected globally; Ad Age covered the event and named Inoue and the video directly.{{sfn|Ad Age|2013}}{{sfn|Saatchi & Saatchi|2013}} He co-directed "Dum Dee Dum" for Keys N Krates with collaborator slavelabour.co,{{sfn|VideoStatic|2013}} and "Run This" for Thugli, the latter with co-director Ohji Inoue through Vision Film Company, with Samy Inayeh as director of photography.

===2014 MuchMusic Video Awards===

Le Blanc's work from 2013 produced an unusual MMVA result the following year. He won Director of the Year for Thugli's "Run This" (co-directed with Inoue), and Dance Video of the Year for Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation".{{sfn|EDM Canada|2014}}{{sfn|Newswire.ca|2014}} "Run This" was also nominated in additional categories, meaning Le Blanc effectively competed against himself on the ballot. Both videos had been produced in cooperation with MuchFACT.

Keys N Krates' "Dum Dee Dum" received a Grand Prize nomination at the Prism Prize and an Official Selection at the Berlin Music Video Awards in the same period.

===Young Director Award===

In 2015, Le Blanc and Inoue won First Prize in the Music Video North America category at the Young Director Award, presented annually at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity.{{sfn|Young Director Award|2015}} The award was for Thugli's "Run This", produced by Vision Film. The video was also shortlisted that year for the 1.4 Brilliant Filmmaking Award, which received over 500 entries.{{sfn|OnepointFour|2015}}

Le Blanc won the Young Director Award a second time for Young Empires' "The Gates" (2015), which reached over 210,000 views on YouTube.{{sfn|shots.net|2016}} A director's cut was released on Vimeo in November 2016.{{sfn|Vimeo|192343074}} The second win was referenced in press coverage around August 2016, when he signed with The Sweet Shop for international commercial representation.

===The Sweet Shop and commercial work===

In August 2016, production company The Sweet Shop announced Le Blanc's signing for international commercial work. The announcement was covered by LBBOnline, shots magazine, adobo Magazine, Campaign Brief, and The Stable, among others.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} Managing Director Laura Thoel of The Sweet Shop Los Angeles was quoted in shots describing his work as "innovative, clever, beautiful and raw."{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

His commercial clients have included Mercedes-Benz, Tesla, Disney, American Express, Budweiser, Air Canada, Beats by Dre, Canon, McDonald's, and Sportchek.

In 2017 he directed the music video for Rudimental featuring James Arthur, "[[Sun Comes Up]]", filmed in South Africa.{{sfn|Sun Comes Up|2017}} In September 2017, Rudimental sat for a [[Audacy|BUILD Series]] interview at BUILD's New York studio specifically about the single.{{sfn|YouTube|WdHxg675dY8}}

===Young Guns===

After what he later described as a period of roughly three years living and working in Southeast Asia, Le Blanc returned to North American work and in November 2019 won in the Film category at the One Club for Creativity's Young Guns 17 awards, held at Sony Hall in New York.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}} The program selected 28 winners from 18 countries across more than 45 countries of submission, judged by a panel of 61 industry figures.{{sfn|SHOOTonline|2019}} Le Blanc was affiliated with Dream Inc., the Toronto-based creative company he co-founded.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}}

LBBOnline published a dedicated article on the win.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2019}}

===Director aliases and production company===
Beyond credits under his given name, Le Blanc has worked under two recurring billing forms. The directorial collective '''slavelabour.co''' (used by Le Blanc with rotating collaborators) holds the directing credit on the Keys N Krates video for "Dum Dee Dum" (2013), the Thugli video for "Sic Em" (2016), and the [[WorldStarHipHop]] Special Feature on [[Michelle Jenneke]] (executive produced by WSHH founder [[Lee O'Denat|Q]]).{{sfn|YouTube|fSTaK8FDnv0}} His production company '''dream inc.''' (owned by Le Blanc, also branded online as thedream.io) holds the production credit on the Thugli video for "Run This" (2013) and the South African hip-hop video for [[Ameen Harron]] featuring K.O.B. SA, "Tonight" (uploaded to the dream inc. official YouTube channel), with Le Blanc credited as both producer and cinematographer.{{sfn|YouTube|ABO3yslNkiI}}

===Music performance and songwriting===
Le Blanc was a member of the Kitchener, Ontario punk rock four-piece '''Mockingbird Wish Me Luck''', which signed to [[Dine Alone Records|Dine Alone Music]] in 2010.{{sfn|Dine Alone Records|nd}}{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}} The label's own roster lists the band among its artists.{{sfn|Dine Alone Records|nd}} Le Blanc contributed as writer, producer, guitarist, and vocalist. The label re-released the band's debut EP ''Goodbye Debris'' digitally in 2010 through Dine Alone Music Inc. (distributed by The Orchard),{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}} with four tracks (''Survival and Defeat'', ''Hollow Graves'', ''Orphans of a Storm'', and ''Brooklyn, NY'') available on Spotify, Bandcamp and YouTube. A follow-up 7" single ''Branches'' (catalogue DAV026, limited to 500 black vinyl) was released on Dine Alone Records on December 14, 2010.{{sfn|Dine Alone Store|2010}} A lyric video for ''Branches'' was uploaded to YouTube in 2011,{{sfn|YouTube|jIUYEIJaHEw}} and live performances from the same era (including ''Orphans of a Storm'' at The Fest 9 in Gainesville, Florida, in November 2010, and a Boxing Day 2009 hometown show in Kitchener) survive in third-party uploads.{{sfn|YouTube|lymGlyOFoto}}{{sfn|YouTube|oqlIp7GN9W8}}{{sfn|YouTube|MWML Goodbye Debris|nd}}

In 2025, Le Blanc provided video production for ''Legacy of Hope'', a tribute song honoring Canadian cancer-research activist [[Terry Fox]] written, produced, recorded, and engineered independently by the band Vital Signz. The band's lineup on the recording included Canadian rock veteran [[Greg Godovitz]] of [[Goddo (band)|Goddo]] on bass guitar and backing vocals, with longtime producer-engineer [[Eddie Kramer]] thanked for drum set-up, mic'ing, and vocal expertise. Additional LeBlanc family members (Nate LeBlanc, Andrea LeBlanc, Stephanie LeBlanc) appear in the song's credits.{{sfn|YouTube|RMyW456kMIA}}

===The Creator Class===
Le Blanc is credited on his own portfolio as the founder, executive producer, and creative director of the documentary platform '''The Creator Class''', launched in 2015.{{sfn|amosleblanc.com|nd}} Individual episodes on the platform's channel do not list those roles as on-screen credits, which is consistent with the structural nature of the role: Le Blanc states he books and casts the creators and partner brands featured across the catalogue without taking an on-camera credit. The platform profiles working creators across photography, art, fashion, and music, with episode catalogues including the five-part ''A NEW BREED'' (2015), ''PHOTO ZERO'', ''THE INFLUENCE'', ''ORIGINALS'', and ''On Fashion And Images''. The official YouTube channel hosts over 80 episodes.{{sfn|YouTube|TCC channel|nd}}

The platform entered into a multi-year partnership with [[Canon Inc.|Canon]] through the '''Canon Creator Lab''' program, providing gear, mentorship, and physical community space for emerging photographers under the hashtag #CANONCREATORLAB.{{sfn|YouTube|4tRZsgxXZI4}} A separate co-branded photo-assignment series ran with [[Red Bull]] Photography from 2017.{{sfn|YouTube|UNI1mve6Q1c}}

The third instalment of ''A NEW BREED'', titled '''Fresh Paint''' (2015), was directed by Le Blanc himself, with cinematography by Christopher Lew and editing by Calum Moore. Filmed at [[Art Basel|Art Basel Miami]], the episode profiles street artists [[Aaron Li-Hill]], [[Adnate]], Cyrcle, Mad Steez, Instagrafite, and Marina Capdevila.{{sfn|YouTube|aW2zJ_pa5FM}}

In January 2022, Le Blanc executive produced and shot a three-part wellness documentary series titled ''EXOTIC QUIXOTIC: Practice Chapters'' through dream inc. Released on the EQ Archives YouTube channel, the chapters cover contact improvisation, rebirthing breathwork, and waterflow (Watsu) therapy.{{sfn|YouTube|ld6KE2zMRkM}}{{sfn|YouTube|Oxse6bkLN7E}}{{sfn|YouTube|1oc9MEBIhy8}}

The platform's broader catalogue has covered work by or about photographers and artists including [[Tyler Mitchell (photographer)|Tyler Mitchell]] (whose [[Vogue (magazine)|''Vogue'']] cover shoot of [[Beyoncé]] is the subject of a dedicated episode), [[Virgil Abloh]] in conversation with photographer Fabien Montique on the ''On Fashion And Images'' panel,{{sfn|YouTube|XmYS9uVzHrc}} [[Sarah Bahbah]] (three episodes including her shoot with [[Noah Centineo]]), [[Alex Strohl]], [[Atiba Jefferson]], [[Olivia Bee]], [[Nadya Kwandibens]], plus profiles touching the shoots of [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Clayton Kershaw]] (the Dodgers no-hitter from the dugout), [[Travis Scott]], [[Playboi Carti]], [[Feist (singer)|Leslie Feist]] and [[Kevin Drew]] of [[Broken Social Scene]], [[Toro y Moi]], and the [[National Ballet of Canada]]. The fourth ''A NEW BREED'' instalment profiles filmmaker Matthew Frost, known for his Instagram shorts featuring [[Lizzy Caplan]], [[Cate Blanchett]], [[Jessica Chastain]], and [[Kirsten Dunst]].

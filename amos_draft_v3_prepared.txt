'''Amos Le Blanc''' is a Canadian film director and commercial director based in [[Los Angeles]] and [[Toronto]], known for music video and commercial production. He has won the [[Young Director Award]] twice at the [[Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity]] and the [[IHeartRadio MMVAs|MuchMusic Video Award]] for Director of the Year in 2014.

==Career==
Le Blanc began his career as a cinematographer on music videos for Canadian artists. His early credits include the music video for [[Islands (band)|Islands]]' "This Is Not A Song", premiered by [[Stereogum]] in December 2011.<ref name="stereogum2011">{{cite web |url=https://www.stereogum.com/898062/islands-this-is-not-a-song-video-stereogum-premiere/news/ |title=Islands – 'This Is Not A Song' Video (Stereogum Premiere) |publisher=Stereogum |date=December 6, 2011}}</ref>

In 2013 he served as producer on Keys N Krates' "Treat Me Right", directed by Ohji Inoue, premiered by EARMILK in March 2013.<ref name="earmilk2013">{{cite web |url=https://earmilk.com/2013/03/06/keys-n-krates-treat-me-right/ |title=Keys N Krates – Treat Me Right |publisher=EARMILK |date=March 6, 2013}}</ref><ref name="themusic2013">{{cite web |url=https://themusic.com.au/reviews/watch-keys-n-krates-treat-me-right-official-video/zqTKwMPCxcQ/20-05-13 |title=Watch: Keys N Krates – Treat Me Right Official Video |publisher=The Music |date=May 20, 2013}}</ref> The video was selected for the 23rd [[Saatchi & Saatchi]] New Directors' Showcase at [[Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity|Cannes Lions]] in June 2013, an event covered by [[Ad Age]] and announced by Saatchi & Saatchi.<ref name="adage2013">{{cite web |url=https://adage.com/article/creativity-at-cannes-2013/richard-dawkins-opens-saatchi-s-2013-directors-showcase/242177 |title=Richard Dawkins Opens Saatchi's 23rd Annual New Directors Showcase |publisher=Ad Age |date=June 18, 2013}}</ref><ref name="saatchi2013">{{cite web |url=https://saatchi.com/en-us/news/presenting_the_23rd_saatchi__saatchi_new_directors_showcase |title=Presenting the 23rd Saatchi & Saatchi New Directors' Showcase |publisher=Saatchi & Saatchi |year=2013}}</ref> He co-directed "Dum Dee Dum" for Keys N Krates under the collective name slavelabour.co, covered by VideoStatic, a trade publication covering music video production.<ref name="videostatic2013">{{cite web |url=https://www.videostatic.com/watch-it/2013/10/18/keys-n-krates-dum-dee-dum-amos-leblanc-slavelabourco-dir |title=Keys N Krates – "Dum Dee Dum" (Amos LeBlanc & slavelabour.co, dir.) |publisher=VideoStatic |date=October 18, 2013}}</ref>

===2014 MuchMusic Video Awards===
At the 2014 [[IHeartRadio MMVAs|MuchMusic Video Awards]], Le Blanc won Director of the Year for Thugli's "Run This" (co-directed with Ohji Inoue through Vision Film) and Dance Video of the Year for Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation".<ref name="edmcanada2014">{{cite web |url=https://www.edmcanada.com/blog/2014/6/16/thugli-and-autoerotique-win-mmva-awards |title=Thugli and Autoerotique win MMVA Awards |publisher=EDM Canada |date=June 16, 2014}}</ref><ref name="newswire2014">{{cite web |url=https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/2014-winners-announced-as-mmva-hosts-mark-a-new-jenner-ation-of-star-power-514456431.html |title=2014 Winners Announced as MMVA Hosts Mark a New Jenner-ation of Star Power |publisher=Newswire.ca |date=June 16, 2014}}</ref>

===Young Director Award===
In 2015, Le Blanc won First Prize in the Music Video North America category at the [[Young Director Award]] at Cannes Lions for Thugli's "Run This".<ref name="yda2015">{{cite web |url=https://www.youngdirectoraward.com/winners-2015/thugli-run-this-173/ |title=Winners 2015: Thugli – Run This |publisher=Young Director Award}}</ref> The video was also shortlisted for the 1.4 Brilliant Filmmaking Award, which received over 500 entries that year.<ref name="14award2015">{{cite web |url=https://www.onepointfour.co/2015/09/09/1-4-shows-long-list-for-brilliant-filmmaking-award/ |title=1.4 Shows Long List for Brilliant Filmmaking Award |publisher=OnepointFour |date=September 9, 2015}}</ref>

Le Blanc won the Young Director Award a second time for Young Empires' "The Gates".<ref name="shots2016">{{cite web |url=https://shots.net/news/view/the-sweet-shop-signs-amos-leblanc |title=The Sweet Shop Signs Amos LeBlanc |publisher=shots.net |date=August 4, 2016}}</ref>

===Commercial representation===
In August 2016, production company [[The Sweet Shop]] announced that Le Blanc had joined its international commercial roster. The signing was covered by LBBOnline, shots magazine, and adobo Magazine, with shots quoting The Sweet Shop Los Angeles managing director Laura Thoel describing his work as "innovative, clever, beautiful and raw".<ref name="lbb2016">{{cite web |url=https://lbbonline.com/news/the-sweet-shop-signs-director-amos-leblanc-2/ |title=The Sweet Shop Signs Director Amos LeBlanc |publisher=LBBOnline |date=August 9, 2016}}</ref><ref name="shots2016"/><ref name="adobo2016">{{cite web |url=https://www.adobomagazine.com/global-news/the-sweet-shop-brings-on-amos-leblanc-for-international-representation/ |title=The Sweet Shop brings on Amos LeBlanc for international representation |publisher=adobo Magazine |date=August 9, 2016}}</ref>

===Young Guns===
In November 2019, Le Blanc won in the Film category at [[The One Club for Creativity]]'s Young Guns 17 awards, held at Sony Hall in New York. The program selected 28 winners from 18 countries across more than 45 countries of submissions, judged by a panel of 61 industry figures.<ref name="oneclub2019">{{cite web |url=https://www.oneclub.org/articles/-view/young-guns-17-amos-le-blanc/ |title=Young Guns 17: Amos Le Blanc |publisher=The One Club for Creativity |date=November 20, 2019}}</ref><ref name="shoot2019">{{cite web |url=https://www.shootonline.com/spw/one-club-announces-28-global-winners-young-guns-17/ |title=One Club Announces 28 Global Winners Young Guns 17 |publisher=SHOOTonline |date=November 2019}}</ref> LBBOnline published a dedicated article on the win.<ref name="lbb2019">{{cite web |url=https://lbbonline.com/news/amos-leblanc-wins-at-the-young-guns |title=Amos Leblanc Wins at the Young Guns |publisher=LBBOnline |date=November 2019}}</ref>

===Climate campaigns===
In September 2021, Le Blanc directed "The Denial" for [[Fridays for Future]] in collaboration with [[Fred & Farid]] Los Angeles, released for the Global Climate Strike. The campaign was covered by shots, LBBOnline, Campaign US, and adobo Magazine.<ref name="shots2021">{{cite web |url=https://shots.net/news/view/fridays-for-future-launches-the-denial |title=Fridays For Future launches The Denial |publisher=shots |date=October 5, 2021}}</ref><ref name="lbb2021">{{cite web |url=https://www.lbbonline.com/news/climate-crisis-denial-is-like-running-into-a-wall-in-fridays-for-future-spot |title=Climate Crisis Denial is Like Running into a Wall in Fridays For Future Spot |publisher=LBBOnline |date=September 21, 2021}}</ref><ref name="campaign2021">{{cite web |url=https://www.campaignlive.com/article/fridays-future-usa-the-denial-fred-farid-los-angeles/1728103 |title=Fridays for Future USA "The Denial" by Fred & Farid Los Angeles |publisher=Campaign US |date=September 21, 2021}}</ref><ref name="adobo2021">{{cite web |url=https://www.adobomagazine.com/campaign-spotlight/campaign-spotlight-for-the-global-climate-strike-september-24-fridays-for-future-u-s-launches-the-denial/ |title=Campaign Spotlight: Fridays For Future U.S. launches "The Denial" for the Global Climate Strike |publisher=adobo Magazine |date=September 22, 2021}}</ref> A follow-up campaign, "We Don't Care", was released in March 2022 and covered by adobo Magazine.<ref name="adobo2022">{{cite web |url=https://www.adobomagazine.com/campaign-spotlight/campaign-spotlight-to-announce-the-next-global-climate-strike-on-march-25-fridays-for-future-u-s-launches-we-dont-care/ |title=Campaign Spotlight: Fridays For Future U.S. launches "We Don't Care" to announce the March 25 Global Climate Strike |publisher=adobo Magazine |date=March 22, 2022}}</ref>

===Music===
Le Blanc was a member of the Kitchener, Ontario punk rock band Mockingbird Wish Me Luck, which signed to [[Dine Alone Records]] in 2010 and released the EP ''Goodbye Debris''. [[Exclaim!]] published a feature on the band at the time of signing.<ref name="exclaim2010">{{cite web |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/mockingbird_wish_me_luck |title=Mockingbird Wish Me Luck |publisher=Exclaim! |date=December 13, 2010}}</ref>

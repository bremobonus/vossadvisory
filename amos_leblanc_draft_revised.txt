'''Amos Le Blanc''' is a Canadian film director and commercial director based in [[Los Angeles]] and [[Toronto]]. He is known primarily for music video direction and commercial production, and has twice won the Young Director Award at [[Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity|Cannes Lions]], in 2015 for Thugli's "Run This" and again the following year for Young Empires' "The Gates". At the 2014 MuchMusic Video Awards, Le Blanc won Director of the Year.

==Early life and education==
Le Blanc was born in 1990 in [[Kitchener, Ontario]].{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}} He began shooting videos at age eleven and was working professionally as a cinematographer on music videos by eighteen.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}}

He enrolled at [[Sheridan College]] in [[Oakville, Ontario]], studying Media Arts, where he transitioned from cinematography to directing.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}}

==Career==

===Early work===
Le Blanc's first noted cinematography credit on a widely released music video was for the Islands track "This Is Not A Song", premiered by [[Stereogum]] in December 2011.{{sfn|Stereogum|2011}}

In 2013 he directed several music videos within the Toronto electronic music scene. He also served as producer on Keys N Krates' "Treat Me Right" (directed by Ohji Inoue), which reached number one on Hype Machine and number one on Beatport's Electronica chart upon release.{{sfn|EARMILK|2013a}} In June 2013 that video was included in the 23rd Saatchi and Saatchi New Directors' Showcase at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, covered by Ad Age.{{sfn|Ad Age|2013}} He co-directed "Dum Dee Dum" for Keys N Krates with collaborator slavelabour.co, a credit noted by the music video trade publication VideoStatic.{{sfn|VideoStatic|2013}}

===2014 MuchMusic Video Awards===
Le Blanc won Director of the Year for Thugli's "Run This" (co-directed with Ohji Inoue) and Dance Video of the Year for Autoerotique's "Asphyxiation" at the 2014 MuchMusic Video Awards.{{sfn|EDM Canada|2014}}{{sfn|Newswire.ca|2014}} Both videos had been produced with the support of MuchFACT.

===Young Director Award===
In 2015, Le Blanc and Inoue won First Prize in the Music Video North America category at the Young Director Award, presented annually at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, for Thugli's "Run This".{{sfn|Young Director Award|2015}}

Le Blanc won the Young Director Award a second time for Young Empires' "The Gates", a win noted in industry coverage around August 2016.{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

===The Sweet Shop===
In August 2016, production company The Sweet Shop announced Le Blanc's signing for international commercial work. The announcement was covered by LBBOnline, shots magazine, and adobo Magazine, among others.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2016}} Managing Director Laura Thoel of The Sweet Shop Los Angeles was quoted in shots describing his work as "innovative, clever, beautiful and raw."{{sfn|shots.net|2016}}

His commercial clients have included Mercedes-Benz, Tesla, Disney, American Express, Budweiser, Air Canada, Beats by Dre, Canon, McDonald's, and Sport Chek.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2016}}

In 2017, Le Blanc directed the music video for Rudimental featuring James Arthur, "[[Sun Comes Up]]", filmed in South Africa.

===Young Guns===
In November 2019, Le Blanc won in the Film category at the One Club for Creativity's Young Guns 17 awards, held at Sony Hall in New York.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}} The program selected 28 winners from 18 countries, judged by a panel of 61 industry figures.{{sfn|SHOOTonline|2019}} Le Blanc was affiliated with Dream Inc., the Toronto-based creative company he co-founded.{{sfn|The One Club|2019}} LBBOnline published a dedicated article on the win.{{sfn|LBBOnline|2019}}

===Fridays for Future===
In 2021, Le Blanc directed "The Denial", a climate-denial PSA released by Fridays for Future for the Global Climate Strike in September 2021, produced through a collaboration between dream inc. and Fred and Farid Los Angeles.{{sfn|shots|2021}}{{sfn|LBBOnline|2021b}}{{sfn|Campaign US|2021}}{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2021}}

In March 2022, Le Blanc directed "We Don't Care" for Fridays for Future U.S., released to announce the March 25 Global Climate Strike.{{sfn|adobo Magazine|2022}}

===Music===
Le Blanc was a member of the Kitchener, Ontario punk rock four-piece Mockingbird Wish Me Luck, which signed to [[Dine Alone Records|Dine Alone Music]] in 2010.{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}} The label re-released the band's debut EP ''Goodbye Debris'' digitally in 2010 through Dine Alone Music Inc. A follow-up 7" single ''Branches'' (catalogue DAV026, limited to 500 black vinyl) was released on Dine Alone Records on December 14, 2010.{{sfn|Exclaim!|2010}}
